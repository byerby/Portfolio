Overview

I had the pleasure to work on a number of different projects while at North Carolina Joint Underwriting Association. The two most notable projects are an entire redesign of the Associations’ website and a custom internal content management system.

The redesign of the website was a project that coincided with the creation of a new portal for all things related to the company. It only made sense that a redesign would be necessary in order to maintain consistency in visual style and efficiency. The redesign was approached with the mindset of increasing navigability, refreshing the visual aesthetic, modernizing the structure, and centralizing key information.

The custom content management system (CMS) was an idea that was born out of frequently requested changes to content on the website. After I pitched the idea to my manager and was granted approval to work on it as a secondary project. Over the course of my time at the company, I gradually learned what I needed to in order to develop a functioning CMS. What began as a way for authorized administrators to access and maintain news posts on the Associations’ grew to allow for the control of content all over the website, such as changes in scheduled board meetings, storm alerts, and contact information, among other things.

My time at NCJUA was a large contributor to what I know and understand about development processes.

Deliverables
Website
This project consisted of a total redesign of the Associations' original website. The new site was intended to bring a more user-friendly interface to customers. The site was commissioned alongside the business's new portal, and later on tied into the content management system.
Content Management System
The CMS was an idea that I brought to the table along with a fellow co-worker. After receiving the green light, I began learning the basics of ASP.NET MVC and C# syntax to implement a CRUD (Create, Read, Update, Delete) application that handled frequently updated information on the website. This was designed to reduce the amount of help desk tickets requesting minor changes on the Associations' website.
