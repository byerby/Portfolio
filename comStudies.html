Overview

The final project for Intro to Graphic Design was commissioned by the Dean of the College of Arts and Sciences. Per the Dean's request, students were to create a design to be printed on a T-Shirt that represents the College of Arts and Sciences. There were around 35 submissions to be judged by two faculty members in the Art department and the Office of the Dean. Out of all of the submissions, I was chosen for having created the winning design.

The thought process behind the T-Shirt design started with a list of words and icons that are representative of the College of Arts and Sciences. After being drawn towards terms relating to critical thinking and the mind, I began brainstorming ways to visually express the mind. A light bulb came to me as the best way to symbolize the mind and critical thinking. Then I began choosing icons to represent each of the 25 departments within the College of Arts and Sciences. Eventually I was able to use the icons and place them accordingly to form the shape of a light bulb.

I chose to pursue this design solution, because from the perspective of the College of Arts and Sciences, all of us students, regardless of the major, have developed the mind of a critical thinker. So, from afar we seem to look as if we are one entity, but it is only when one takes a closer look that they realize we are very diverse in our skills and knowledge.
