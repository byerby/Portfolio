Overview

RosterIQ is my thesis project that capped off my graduate studies in the Master of Arts in Interactive Media program at Elon University.

RosterIQ is an interactive map of athletic rosters. The idea was born out of my own curiosity about where college football players come from. It was my hope that the application could be used to discover trends and patterns in recruiting. The current state of the project plots the hometowns of players from the years 2007 to 2013. Also, it currently only is using Southeastern Conference data. There are also options to filter by team and player position.

There was no certainty in whether or not my creation would result in a web application that could help the discovery of trends in recruiting. However, with data to support it and the potential to approach the geospatial visualizations from different perspectives there is room for scalability to other sports. In the long run, I hope to continue development on this project to open new doors of possibilities for its use.
