Homey
UX/UI Designer

Homey is a mobile application to help the homeless connect with helpful services and each other. The idea began with the question of who has a need that can be satisfied with mobile application technology. After accumulating a list of about ten user groups, we decided that homeless individuals have many needs that they seek to meet. The application was moved through conceptualization to mock-up by myself and two other colleagues. The project was a part of my graduate studies, but has the potential to be a viable product that may have an impact on homeless around the country.

Our application design focused primarily on cleanliness and friendliness. In our attempt to strive for those characteristics we decided that green and white were going to be our primary brand colors. In addition, designing for small screens was something we had to really focus on, because we are not afforded the space that a desktop provides. So, we had to get creative with our navigation and interaction designs.

I plan to continue the development on this project and to see it through to a functional product.
